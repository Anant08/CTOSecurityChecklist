Your company

  • Ensure your domain names are secured

    Seed

    Domain names should be renewed regularly. If you bought it from a third party, you should also make sure the authoritative configured name server is your own.

  • Have a public security policy

    Series A

    This is a page on your corporate website, describing how you plan to respond to external bug reports. You should advise you support responsible disclosure.

    https://www.airbnb.com/security https://www.apple.com/support/security/

  • Have an internal security policy

    Above

    This is a short document stating of the security requirements in your company, and defining who are the actors of the security.

    https://www.sans.org/reading-room/whitepapers/policyissues/creating-information-systems-security-policy-534

  • Use a bug bounty program

    Series A

    A bug bounty program will allow external people to report you vulnerabilities for money. You need some security aware people inside your company to balance the reports you will get (and nothing is more alarming than a security report).

    https://bountyfactory.io/ https://hackerone.com/

  • Be honest and transparent about the data you may collect

    Seed

    In case of a breach, people will disclose the data they gathered. Your customers need to be aware of what data you're storing.

  • Do not share Wifi

    Series A

    Sharing Wifi networks with guests or neighbors may give them the opportunity to gather information on your network, and to access IP granted resources. Use a guest dedicated Wifi network.
